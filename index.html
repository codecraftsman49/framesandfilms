Rediscovering the Joy of Shooting the Film Photography

After spending a decade focused solely on making a career from digital, I've recently picked up some film and rediscovered the joy of an analogue shutter.

John Doe

20 Jan 2024

Design Photography

In the frenetic world of digital photography, where the pursuit of perfection often overshadows the journey itself, there lies a quiet revolution—a rekindling of passion and reverence for the timeless art of shooting on film. For years, I was swept up in the allure of digital cameras, chasing megapixels and pixels-per-inch in pursuit of the perfect shot. Yet, despite the advancements in technology and the convenience of instant gratification, something was missing—a sense of connection, of authenticity, that I found myself yearning for.


As I delved deeper into the world of digital photography, honing my skills and building a career, I couldn't shake the feeling that I had lost touch with the essence of what drew me to photography in the first place. It wasn't until recently, after stumbling upon an old film camera tucked away in a dusty corner of a thrift store, that I was reminded of the magic and allure of shooting on film.




"You don't take a photograph, you make it."

- Ansel Adams

With a sense of curiosity and nostalgia, I loaded a roll of film into the camera and ventured out into the world, eager to rediscover the joy of analogue photography. With each click of the shutter, I found myself immersed in the moment, attuned to the rhythm of light and shadow, color and texture. Gone were the days of mindlessly snapping away, hoping for a decent shot. Instead, every frame became a precious opportunity to capture a fleeting moment in time, to distill emotion and memory onto a single frame of film.


Shooting on film also reconnected me with the rich history and tradition of photography, allowing me to tap into a lineage that stretches back to the earliest days of the medium. With each roll of film, I became part of a legacy—a custodian of an art form that has endured the test of time. There's a certain reverence that comes with shooting on film, a respect for the craft and the countless photographers who came before me, paving the way for future generations.


Despite the prevalence of digital technology, film photography continues to thrive, attracting a new generation of photographers who crave the tactile experience and artistic freedom that shooting on film provides. In a world that often feels ephemeral and transient, film photography offers a sense of permanence—a tangible connection to the past and a reminder of the enduring power of the photographic image.



Software and tools


While film photography primarily relies on traditional cameras and film stocks, there are still digital tools available for scanning and post-processing film images. Software such as Adobe Photoshop and Lightroom can be used to enhance and edit scanned film images, allowing photographers to achieve their desired aesthetic while preserving the inherent qualities of film.



Other resources


For those interested in delving deeper into the world of film photography, there are numerous online communities, workshops, and tutorials available.

Websites such as Reddit's r/analog and forums like APUG (Analog Photography Users Group) provide valuable resources and support for film enthusiasts of all skill levels.

Additionally, local camera shops and photography clubs often host events and classes focused on film photography, offering opportunities for hands-on learning and networking with like-minded individuals.



Conclusion


In a digital age where technology continues to redefine the boundaries of photography, film photography remains a timeless and enduring medium. With its emphasis on intentionality, craftsmanship, and connection to the past, shooting on film offers a unique and rewarding experience for photographers of all backgrounds. Whether you're a seasoned professional or an amateur enthusiast, rediscovering the joy of shooting film photography can reignite your passion for the craft and open up new avenues for creative expression.
